Grace: The Young Girl Who Dared to Resist Child Early Forced Marriage

Date: June 1, 2023

Like millions of other girls in South Sudan, Grace lived in daily fear that one day she would be forced out of school and into an early marriage. According to a study by the Strategic Initiative for Women in the Horn of Africa, over 50% of girls in South Sudan are married before the age of 18, causing many to drop out of school.

Choosing a Different Path

At just 15, Grace’s fears came close to reality after her first menstrual period, which in many South Sudanese communities signifies a girl's readiness for marriage. Her father insisted she drop out of school and get married. When she refused, he used physical violence, which alarmed the community.

Thankfully, the local Women Protection Team (WPT) intervened. Their chairlady rescued Grace and helped her return to school in town. This moment gave her a chance to continue her education and escape the trap of forced marriage.

The Nonviolent Peaceforce (NP) had launched the WPT in Aguka in 2021. Through mentoring sessions and awareness campaigns, the team empowered girls and women to resist violence and forced marriage. Grace met the WPT during these activities and was inspired by their mission.

Advocating for Change

Grace, now preparing for her final national exams, says, “I feel listened to, I feel confident in myself and I have peace of mind. I know I will pass my final exams and go to university.”

Her teacher added, “Grace is a very active student. She is willing to learn and will be a great asset to her community if she continues her education.”

Motivated by her experience, Grace joined the WPT as its youngest member to help girls like herself resist child marriage. “They helped me so much, I like what they do, and I also want to help other girls.”

Her message to others: “Resist the pressure of early marriage. Believe that your goals can be achieved. Express yourself and make people hear you.”
