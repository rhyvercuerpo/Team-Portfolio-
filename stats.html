Comparative Number of VAWC Cases (Line Graph)

The line graph titled "Comparative Number of VAWC Cases" shows the trend in reported cases of Violence Against Women and Children (VAWC) from 2022 to 2024. The graph reveals a steady decline in the number of cases over the three-year period. In 2022, there were approximately 470 reported cases, which slightly decreased to around 450 in 2023. By 2024, the number of cases further dropped to about 400.

Distribution of Types of Violence (CY 2022)

Distribution of Types of Violence (CY 2022) The pie chart for Calendar Year 2022 presents the percentage breakdown of different types of Violence Against Women and Children (VAWC) cases. The most commonly reported type of violence was economic/financial abuse, accounting for 32% of the total cases. This was followed by emotional/psychological abuse at 28%, physical abuse at 17%, and child custody-related cases at 14%. Sexual abuse represented 7% of the cases, while trafficking in persons was the least reported at only 1%. The chart clearly highlights that non-physical forms of abuse, particularly economic and emotional abuse, were more prevalent in 2022. Distribution of Types of Violence (CY 2024)

Distribution of Types of Violence (CY 2024) The doughnut chart for Calendar Year 2024 displays the updated distribution of violence types in VAWC cases. A notable observation is the significant decrease in physical abuse, which dropped to just 2.2% of reported cases. While the exact percentages for other categories are not all labeled, the chart suggests a continued prevalence of emotional/psychological and economic abuse, though their proportions may have shifted slightly since 2022.

Labor Force Participation Rate in the Philippines by Region
July 2021, April 2022, and July 2022

This shows the labor force participation rate in the Philippines by region for July 2021, April 2022, and July 2022. The graph clearly illustrates that labor force participation is not evenly distributed across the country, with some regions experiencing consistently higher rates than others. Region IV-A (CALABARZON) stands out with the highest labor force participation rate in one of the recorded months, while BARMM (Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao) shows persistently lower levels compared to other regions. These figures indicate that a significant portion of the working-age population in these areas may be actively employed or seeking employment. On the other hand, regions such as BARMM have lower labor force participation rates, suggesting potential challenges in labor market engagement.

Underemployment Rate in the Philippines by Region
January 2023, October 2023, and January 2024

This shows the underemployment rate in the Philippines by region for January 2023, October 2023, and January 2024. The graph clearly illustrates that underemployment is not evenly distributed across the country, with some regions experiencing consistently higher rates than others. Region VIII stands out with the highest underemployment rate, reaching close to 30% in one of the recorded months, while BARMM (Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao) also shows persistently high levels. These figures indicate that a significant portion of the labor force in these areas may be engaged in jobs that are insufficient in terms of hours, income, or skill match. On the other hand, regions such as the National Capital Region (NCR) and Region IV-A (CALABARZON) have lower underemployment rates, suggesting better job quality and more stable employment opportunities.
